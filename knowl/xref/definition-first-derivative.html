Definition 3.3.1. The First Derivative Function.

If \(f\) is a function of \(x\text{,}\) then we define the first derivative function, \(\fd{f}\text{,}\) as
\begin{equation*} \fe{\fd{f}}{x}=\lim_{h\to0}\frac{\fe{f}{x+h}-\fe{f}{x}}{h}\text{.} \end{equation*}
The symbols \(\fe{\fd{f}}{x}\) are read aloud as “\(f\) prime of \(x\)” or “\(f\) prime at \(x\text{.}\)”

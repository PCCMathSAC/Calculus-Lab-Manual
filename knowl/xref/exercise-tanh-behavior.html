Exercise 9.

Let \(\fe{f}{t}=\fe{\tanh}{t}\text{.}\) What is the sign on \(\fe{\fd{f}}{t}\) at all values of \(t\text{?}\) What does this tell you about the function \(f\text{?}\) What are the values of \(\fe{f}{0}\) and \(\fe{\fd{f}}{0}\text{?}\)
Answer.
positive; \(f\) is always increasing; \(\fe{f}{0}=0\text{;}\) \(\fe{\fd{f}}{0}=1\)
Solution.
Since \(\fe{\fd{f}}{t}=\fe{\sech^2}{t}\text{,}\) \(\fe{\fd{f}}{t}\) is positive for all values of \(t\text{.}\) So \(f\) is always increasing. And we can calculate
\begin{align*} \fe{f}{0}&=\fe{\tanh}{0}&\fe{\fd{f}}{0}&=\fe{\sech^2}{0}\\ &=\frac{e^0-e^{-0}}{e^0+e^{-0}}&&=\frac{2}{e^0+e^{-0}}\\ &=\frac{0}{2}&&=\frac{2}{2}\\ &=0&&=1 \end{align*}

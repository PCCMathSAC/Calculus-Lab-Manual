Example 2.3.1.

\begin{align*} \lim_{x\to3}\frac{x^2-8x+15}{x-3}&=\lim_{x\to3}\frac{(x-5)(x-3)}{x-3}\\ &=\lim_{x\to3}(x-5)&&\knowl{./knowl/xref/lla7.html}{\text{Theorem A.0.10}}\\ &=\lim_{x\to3}x-\lim_{x\to3}5&&\knowl{./knowl/xref/lla2.html}{\text{Theorem A.0.5}}\\ &=3-5&&\knowl{./knowl/xref/llr1.html}{\text{Theorem A.0.1}}, \knowl{./knowl/xref/llr2.html}{\text{R2 A.0.2}}\\ &=-2 \end{align*}
Note that the forms of the limits on either side of the first line are \(\frac{0}{0}\text{,}\) but the form of the limit in the second line is no longer indeterminate. So at the second line, we can begin applying limit laws.

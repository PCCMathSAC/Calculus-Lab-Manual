Example 2.3.2.

\begin{align*} \lim_{\theta\to0}\frac{1-\fe{\cos}{\theta}}{\fe{\sin^2}{\theta}}&=\lim_{\theta\to0}\left(\frac{1-\fe{\cos}{\theta}}{\fe{\sin^2}{\theta}}\cdot\frac{1+\fe{\cos}{\theta}}{1+\fe{\cos}{\theta}}\right)\\ &=\lim_{\theta\to0}\frac{1-\fe{\cos^2}{\theta}}{\fe{\sin^2}{\theta}\cdot\left(1+\fe{\cos}{\theta}\right)}\\ &=\lim_{\theta\to0}\frac{\fe{\sin^2}{\theta}}{\fe{\sin^2}{\theta}\cdot\left(1+\fe{\cos}{\theta}\right)}\\ &=\lim_{\theta\to0}\frac{1}{1+\fe{\cos}{\theta}}&&\knowl{./knowl/xref/lla7.html}{\text{Theorem A.0.10}}\\ &=\frac{\lim_{\theta\to0}1}{\lim_{\theta\to0}\left(1+\fe{\cos}{\theta}\right)}&&\knowl{./knowl/xref/lla5.html}{\text{Theorem A.0.8}}\\ &=\frac{\lim_{\theta\to0}1}{\lim_{\theta\to0}1+\lim_{\theta\to0}\fe{\cos}{\theta}}&&\knowl{./knowl/xref/lla1.html}{\text{Theorem A.0.4}}\\ &=\frac{\lim_{\theta\to0}1}{\lim_{\theta\to0}1+\fe{\cos}{\lim_{\theta\to0}\theta}}&&\knowl{./knowl/xref/lla6.html}{\text{Theorem A.0.9}}\\ &=\frac{1}{1+\fe{\cos}{0}}&&\knowl{./knowl/xref/llr1.html}{\text{Theorem A.0.1}}, \knowl{./knowl/xref/llr2.html}{\text{R2 A.0.2}}\\ &=\frac{1}{2} \end{align*}
The form of each limit above was \(\frac{0}{0}\) until the fourth line, where we were able to begin applying limit laws.

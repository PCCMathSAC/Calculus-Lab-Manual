Exercise Group 3.5.7–10.

Determine the unit on the first derivative function for each of the following functions. Remember, we do not simplify derivative units in any way, shape, or form.

7.

\(\fe{R}{p}\) is Carl’s heart rate (beats per min) when he jogs at a rate of \(p\) (measured in ft⁄min).
Answer.
\(\frac{\sfrac{\text{beats}}{\text{min}}}{\sfrac{\text{ft}}{\text{min}}}\)
Solution.
The unit for \(\fe{\fd{R}}{p}\) is \(\frac{\sfrac{\text{beats}}{\text{min}}}{\sfrac{\text{ft}}{\text{min}}}\text{.}\)

8.

\(\fe{F}{v}\) is the fuel consumption rate gal⁄mi of Hanh’s pick-up when she drives it on level ground at a constant speed of \(v\) (measured in mi⁄h).
Answer.
\(\frac{\sfrac{\text{gal}}{\text{mi}}}{\sfrac{\text{mi}}{\text{hr}}}\)
Solution.
The unit for \(\fe{\fd{F}}{v}\) is \(\frac{\sfrac{\text{gal}}{\text{mi}}}{\sfrac{\text{mi}}{\text{hr}}}\text{.}\)

9.

\(\fe{v}{t}\) is the velocity of the space shuttle mi⁄h where \(t\) is the amount of time that has passed since liftoff (measured in s).
Answer.
\(\frac{\sfrac{\text{mi}}{\text{hr}}}{\text{s}}\)
Solution.
The unit for \(\fe{\fd{v}}{t}\) is \(\frac{\sfrac{\text{mi}}{\text{hr}}}{\text{s}}\text{.}\)

10.

\(\fe{h}{t}\) is the elevation of the space shuttle (mi) where \(t\) is the amount of time that has passed since lift-off (measured in s).
Answer.
mi⁄s
Solution.
The unit for \(\fe{\fd{h}}{t}\) is mi⁄s.

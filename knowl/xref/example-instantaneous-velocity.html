Example 3.1.1.

\begin{align*} \fe{v}{2}&=\lim_{h\to0}\frac{\fe{s}{2+h}-\fe{s}{2}}{h}\\ &=\lim_{h\to0}\frac{\left[40+40\left(2+h\right)-16\left(2+h\right)^2\right]-\left[40+40(2)-16(2)^2\right]}{h}\\ &=\lim_{h\to0}\frac{40+80+40h-64-64h-16h^2-56}{h}\\ &=\lim_{h\to0}\frac{-24h-16h^2}{h}\\ &=\lim_{h\to0}\frac{h\left(-24-16h\right)}{h}\\ &=\lim_{h\to0}\left(-24-16h\right)\\ &=-24-16\cdot0\\ &=-24 \end{align*}
From this we can infer that the velocity of the object \(2\) seconds into its flight is -24 ft⁄s. From that we know that the object is falling at a speed of 24 ft⁄s.
